# HSE hazard bank by activity

Use this as a starting bank. Always tailor to the actual site conditions and the project's risk assessment — this is not a substitute for a competent HSE review.

## General site hazards (apply to most activities)
- Slips, trips, and falls — housekeeping, cable management, edge protection.
- Manual handling — team lifts, mechanical aids, weight limits.
- Working at height — scaffolds inspected/tagged, harnesses, guardrails.
- Dust and airborne particles — extraction, wetting down, RPE.
- Electrical — inspected tools, RCDs, no damaged leads.
- Noise — hearing protection in high-noise zones.
- Housekeeping & waste — segregated bins, clear access/egress.

## Blockwork / masonry
- Cement/mortar contact — skin irritation/burns → gloves, wash stations.
- Manual handling of blocks → team lifts, staged deliveries.
- Falling materials from scaffold → toe boards, exclusion zones.

## Gypsum board / drywall partitions & false ceilings
- Cutting dust (silica) → low-dust methods, RPE, extraction.
- Working at height for ceilings → mobile scaffold towers, tagged.
- Sharp edges / fixings → gloves, safe blade discipline.

## Screeding / plastering
- Cement contact and eye splash → PPE, eyewash.
- Wet floors → signage, controlled access, curing barriers.
- Repetitive strain → rotation, ergonomic tools.

## Waterproofing
- Solvent/primer fumes → ventilation, RPE, no ignition sources.
- Hot works (torch-on membranes) → hot-work permit, fire watch, extinguishers.
- Confined/wet areas → ventilation and access controls.

## Tiling / finishing
- Adhesive/grout dust and skin contact → PPE.
- Cutting (wet saws) → guards, electrical safety near water.
- Kneeling strain → knee protection, rotation.

## MEP first/second fix
- Electrical isolation / lock-out-tag-out before work.
- Working at height for containment/ductwork.
- Pressure testing hazards → controlled test areas, gauges.
- Hot works for pipe joining → permit and fire watch.
