# Method Statement — annotated structure & worked example

## Annotated outline

Each method statement is a controlled document. Keep the header/footer consistent and version-controlled.

### Header block (every page)
- Project name and contract number
- Document title: "Method Statement — <Activity>"
- Document number (placeholder, e.g. `MS-<disc>-<seq>`), revision, date
- Page x of y

### 1. Purpose & scope
State the activity covered and where it applies (areas, floors, zones). Note explicitly what is excluded to avoid scope creep.

### 2. References
List with document numbers where possible:
- Contract drawings (architectural, structural, MEP as relevant)
- Specification sections (e.g. "Section 04 20 00 — Unit Masonry")
- Approved material submittals
- Approved shop drawings
- Standards & codes
- Project HSE plan and quality plan

### 3. Responsibilities
- **Project/Construction Manager** — overall delivery, resources.
- **Site Engineer** — setting out, execution to drawings, raising WIRs.
- **Foreman/Charge-hand** — supervising the crew, sequence, workmanship.
- **QA/QC Engineer** — ITP, inspections, non-conformance handling.
- **HSE Officer** — risk assessment, toolbox talks, PPE, permits.

### 4. Resources
- **Manpower**: table of trade vs count (indicative).
- **Plant & equipment**: mixers, hoists, scaffolds, power tools, lasers/levels.
- **Materials**: reference approved submittals; do not list unapproved substitutes.

### 5. Sequence of works
Numbered chronological steps. Group into: **Preparation → Execution → Protection/Curing → Cleaning & handover**. Reference hold points where a WIR is required.

### 6. Inspection & Test Plan (ITP) hooks
Define hold (H), witness (W), and surveillance (S) points. Typical: material verification, pre-pour/pre-close inspection, in-progress workmanship, final inspection.

### 7. HSE
Reference the activity risk assessment. Summarise top hazards and controls. See `hse-hazards.md`.

### 8. Quality control
Tolerances and acceptance criteria drawn from the spec (e.g. verticality ±, surface regularity under a 2 m straightedge, adhesion, coverage). Tie every criterion to a spec clause or standard.

## Worked example — Internal Blockwork (indicative sequence)

1. Ensure area is handed over, slab cleaned, and setting-out approved (WIR — hold point).
2. Mark wall lines and openings per architectural drawings; verify against MEP for penetrations.
3. Fix starter/dowel bars and wall ties as per structural detail.
4. Lay damp-proof course where required.
5. Prepare mortar to approved mix; do not retemper set mortar.
6. Lay first course on a full mortar bed; check line, level, and gauge.
7. Build up in courses; maintain verticality (plumb) and horizontality (level) — check every few courses.
8. Install control/movement joints at specified centres.
9. Build in lintels over openings with specified bearing.
10. Leave for curing; protect fresh work from impact and rapid drying.
11. Raise WIR for consultant inspection before plastering/closing (hold point).

Adapt the sequence to the actual activity (screed, tiling, gypsum partitions, waterproofing, MEP first fix, etc.).
